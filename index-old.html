Hi, I'm Chris Kobar and for more than a dozen years I have been helping clients achieve their business goals by providing direction and hands-on expertise in the design and creation of simple, intuitive, consistent, beautiful, and effective user experiences.

An illustrator and designer since childhood, I understand the incredible power of a compelling visual experience to attract and delight users, build customer admiration and loyalty, and establish a strong brand in the marketplace. However, without the structural, behavioral, and informational front-end components that frame, drive, and bring the larger user experience to life, the visual presentation is little more than eye-candy. And in order to achieve these things efficiently and consistently, the right technologies, tools, and workflows are essential.

Knowing what these elements are and being able to properly implement them is no small thing. The days of hiring a freelance designer to create hi-fidelity Photoshop mockups, an information architect to manufacture dozens of detailed wireframes, and a developer to write code that only works for Internet Explorer on screens at least 960 pixels wide, are no more. Today, cross-discipline teams utilizing rough sketches, style tiles, front-end frameworks, in-browser design prototypes, agnostic development, and agile workflows are the norm.

Organizing, mentoring and providing direction to these creative and technical teams to ensure their production is greater than the sum of their parts requires a leader who has been there and done that; someone able to work closely with executives and stakeholders to make sure they are effectively informed and involved; and someone who can do it with professional integrity and character.

I have been providing that direction, leadership, and expertise to large enterprises seeking to transform burdensome legacy applications into modern, mobile-friendly, interactive service touchpoints for their constituents and customers on a local and national level. Numerous smaller clients have also benefited from my assistance with visual and interaction design, front-end development, information architecture, storyboarding, usability testing, user research, content and digital strategy, copy writing, art direction, and branding and identity design.

I'm Chris Kobar and I'm a UX designer.

Colophon

Site structure and style is built on Zurb's Foundation front-end framework, with a little help from Sass and Compass. Interaction is powered by the ubiquitous jQuery Javascript library, with extra magic provided by Faruk Ateş's essential Modernizr, Nick Williams' handy Enquire, Tyler Gaw's Jribbble, Imulus's helpful RetinaJS, and Abban Dunne's jQuery Picture. The content is managed by the deviously slick Statamic, the brainchild of Jack McDade, Fred LeBlanc, and Mubs Iqbal.

Initial design was done in Adobe's Illustrator CC and Photoshop CC. Coding was done in Adobe's Edge Code CC, processed with Subash Pathak's truly genius Prepros, and developed in the Chrome and Safari webkit browsers. Exljbris Font Foundry's Museo is used for primary headings, while The League of Moveable Type's Raleway is used for the body and lesser headings. Both are served up by Adobe's Typekit via their Creative Cloud service and loaded with Google's Webfont Loader.
